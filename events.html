Events from our Second Season, 2016 - 2017


24 March 2017: Great Lakes Baroque presents Suzanne Lommler, mezzo-soprano

In recent seasons, brilliant young mezzo-soprano Suzanne Lommler has garnered rave reviews for her performances with Chicago's Haymarket Opera Company. With Jory Vinikour on harpsichord and fortepiano and Paul Dwyer on Baroque cello, Suzanne performed music by Georg Friedrich Händel, Franz Joseph Haydn, and Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart.

Friday, 24 March 2017, 7:00 PM
North Shore Congregational Church
7330 North Santa Monica Boulevard
Fox Point, WI 53217


18 November 2016: Fireworks for Two Harpsichords!

Celebrating the release of their Delos recording of Padre Antonio Soler's Six Concerti for Two Harpsichords, Great Lakes Baroque founders Philippe LeRoy and Jory Vinikour performed music for two harpsichords by Soler, Wilhelm Friedemann Bach, Johann Christian Bach, and other composers.

Friday, 18 November 2016, 7:00 PM
North Shore Congregational Church
7330 North Santa Monica Boulevard
Fox Point, WI 53217

28 February 2016: From Westminster to Wisconsin - If music be the food of love

Featuring songs and duets by Henry Purcell (1659 - 1695), If music be the food of love united sopranos Chelsea Morris and Erica Schuller with the instrumental trio of David Walker (theorbo), Craig Trompeter (Baroque cello and viola da gamba), and Great Lakes Baroque founder Jory Vinikour (harpsichord) in performances of music by England's greatest composer of the Seventeenth Century.

Sunday, 28 February 2016, 5:00 PM
North Shore Congregational Church
7330 North Santa Monica Boulevard
Fox Point, WI 53217


13 September 2015: Io vidi in terra

Presenting a programme of Italian songs and duets from the Seventeenth Century, Io vidi in terra brought internationally-renowned soloists mezzo-soprano Céline Ricci and countertenor José Lemos together with Deborah Fox on theorbo and Baroque guitar, Baroque cellist Craig Trompeter, and harpsichordist Jory Vinikour. The music of Claudio Monteverdi, Marco da Gagliano, Barbara Strozzi, Tarquinio Merula, Agostino Steffani, and other composers resounded in Great Lakes Baroque's first concert.

Sunday, 13 September 2015, 7:30 PM
St. Joseph's Convent Chapel
Milwaukee, WI 53215
